North Indian Architecture

North Indian architecture encompasses Indo-Islamic, Mughal, and Rajput styles.


The Taj Mahal


The Taj Mahal is an iconic masterpiece of Mughal architecture located in Agra, India. Constructed by Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century, it was dedicated to his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal. The Taj Mahal is renowned for its exquisite white marble craftsmanship, intricate floral patterns, and delicate inlay work of semi-precious stones. Its symmetrical design features a central dome flanked by four minarets, reflecting in a serene pool. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage site, the Taj Mahal stands as a symbol of eternal love and architectural grandeur.



The Qutub Minar


The Qutub Minar is a remarkable monument located in Delhi, India. Standing at a height of around 73 meters (240 feet), it is the world's tallest brick minaret. Built in the 12th century, it is an exquisite example of Indo-Islamic architecture and is part of the Qutub complex, a UNESCO World Heritage site. The minar is adorned with intricate carvings, verses from the Quran, and decorative balconies. Its construction was initiated by Qutb-ud-din Aibak and completed by Iltutmish, showcasing the influence of the Delhi Sultanate. The Qutub Minar stands as a testament to India's rich cultural heritage and architectural prowess.



The India Gate


India Gate is a prominent monument located in the heart of New Delhi, India. It is a war memorial built in memory of Indian soldiers who lost their lives during World War I and the Afghan Wars. Designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens, it stands as a symbol of national pride and sacrifice. The monument features an imposing archway made of red sandstone, adorned with inscriptions and the names of soldiers. Surrounded by lush green lawns, it serves as a popular gathering place and a tribute to the bravery of Indian soldiers. India Gate is an iconic landmark that holds great historical and patriotic significance for the country.
